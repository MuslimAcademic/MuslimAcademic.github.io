Free Arabic Fonts

Resource List

Published: 27 Safar 1442 / 14 October 2020

Last Updated: 2 Rajab 1442 / 14 February 2021

A list of resources for Arabic fonts that are free to use for personal, commercial, and non-commercial purposes.

  • Google Fonts - Early Access
    This page has direct download links for Arabic Noto fonts, which are under Apache License v2.0, and also to the regular Arabic Google Fonts.
    • Google Noto Fonts
      Type "Arabic" in the search bar and you'll get the fonts in the search results. Fonts are licensed under under Apache License v2.0.
    • Google Fonts
      The largest resource for 'free for commercial use' Arabic fonts. Mostly (probably all of them) licensed under the Open Font License.

  • King Fahad Quran Printing Complex Fonts (Arabic website)
    The fonts available on this site are copyrighted, BUT, full permission is given to distribute and use for personal and commercial purposes, on the condition that it is not attributed to anyone other than them. Permission is not given to modify or sell the font itself.
    (Interesting fact, the actual Quran that they print is completely handwritten and does not use any font.)
    • If you actually want to type out the Quran (or a part of it), then you can download their old Quran fonts. There are 2 sets of these, the 2005 version and the 2013 version. Both of them contain 605 font files each. 604 font files for each page of the Quran and 1 for "bismillah" and some other things like Surah names, etc. So there is a separate font file for each page, and each character of each font gives you 1 word of that page, meaning that you cannot type letter-by-letter like you normally would, instead you type "word-by-word".
      These were available on the official website until mid-2019, now the website offers Photoshop PSD files instead.

  • SIL International Fonts
    Most of these are included in Google Fonts, but 1 or 2 aren't. They are licensed under the SIL Open Font License.

  • Font Squirrel
    Normally this is the 1st website I go to for free fonts, but it only has 2 Arabic fonts and they're both available in Google Fonts as well.

  • Expired DecoType Fonts
    More recent DecoType fonts are still copyrighted, however a series of early DecoType fonts (Copyrighted 1992 - 1998) that were licensed to users of Microsoft Office in the Middle East are now expired and have entered Public Domain, which means that they are completely free to use for both personal and commercial use.
