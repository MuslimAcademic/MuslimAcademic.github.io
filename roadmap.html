What is the Path?

A Summary

What is the path to seeking Islamic knowledge?

The Islamic Sciences are divided into 2 categories:
  • The Objective Sciences (علوم الغاية)
  • The Helping Sciences (علوم الآلة)
Each category has 4 subjects

The Objective Sciences are:
  1. Qur'an (including tafsir)
  2. Hadith
  3. Fiqh
  4. Aqeedah
The Helping Sciences are:
  1. The Quranic Sciences (علوم القرآن)
  2. Hadith Classification (مصطلح الحديث)
  3. The Principles of Fiqh (أصول الفقه)
  4. Arabic
The Objective Sciences are those subjects which your main objective is to know more about them,
so you want to understand:
  • the Quran
  • the Sunnah of the Prophet [Hadith]
  • things which are implied in the Quran and Hadith or derived from them [Fiqh]
[ Aqeedah is actually included within the Quran and Hadith, but because of it's importance, and just to show it a degree respect, it is classified as a separate subject ]

The Helping Sciences are those subjects which help you to understand the objective sciences.
So for example, learning hadith classification helps you understand which ahadith are authentic, learning Arabic helps you understand the Quran and Hadith, etc.

Within each subject there is an established order of books to study.
So for example, for hadith you study in this order:
  1. Imam Nawawi's 40 Ahadith
  2. Umdatul-Ahkaam or Buluughul Maraam
  3. The 6 well known books of hadith
For Aqeedah you study in this order:
  1. The 3 Fundamental Principles
  2. The 4 Principles
  3. Kashf-ush-Shubuhaat
  4. Kitaab-ut-Tawhid
  5. Al-Aqeedat-ul-Waasitiyyah
For Arabic you study:
  1. Al-Ajurrumiyyah
  2. Qatr-un-Nada
  3. Alfiyyah Ibn Maalik
etc etc

Sometimes different Sheikhs might recommend something slightly different, but it's generally the same within each region. So for example in Makkah, Madinah, and in Saudi Arabia in general, they follow what I just mentioned. South Asian madrasas etc have their own system. Egypt has their own system, etc etc.

But for now I'll just tell you the one i know of, which is what they follow here in Saudi Arabia.

So when it comes to what you should start with, generally all scholars advice that: You start with that which you need to know.

Which means 2 things:
  • Aqeedah
  • Fiqh-ul-Ibaadaat
(If, for example, you're a businessman, then you should also study the relevant fiqh etc, but these 2 are basically the essentials for everyone).

Fiqh has 4 parts:
  • Ibaadaat (worship)
  • Buyuu' (business)
  • Nikah/Usra (Marriage/Family)
  • Jinayaat (criminal law)
and the 1st part is something which is really important for every person to know:
how to pray, how to fast, etc.
